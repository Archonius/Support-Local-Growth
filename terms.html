Terms & Conditions

Last updated: July 2026.

This is a general-purpose terms document written to reflect the service as described: pricing, eligibility and the fair-use edit policy. It is not a substitute for legal advice, and it does not attempt to fully address UK consumer contract law (including distance-selling cancellation rights). Have it reviewed by a solicitor before relying on it for a live, trading business.

1. The service

Support Local Growth designs and builds standard brochure websites for eligible independent UK small businesses, for a flat one-off build fee of £250. Optional ongoing hosting is available on a monthly rolling basis (Standard £15/month, Pro £30/month), or you may take the Self-Hosted option and host the finished files yourself at no ongoing cost.

2. Eligibility

The scheme is available only to businesses that are UK-based, independently owned (not a franchise, subsidiary or branch of a larger group), have fewer than 25 employees, and have under £150,000 annual net profit. We do not build for gambling, adult content, political campaigning, or illegal businesses, and we only build standard informational brochure sites — not ecommerce, booking systems, member logins, or bespoke web applications. Full detail is on our Eligibility & FAQ page. We may decline, pause or discontinue an order at any point if a business does not meet these criteria, including where this only becomes apparent after work has started.

3. Ordering process

You order by completing our questionnaire and choosing a hosting plan. Payment is processed by Stripe. Once payment is confirmed, we build your site to the specifications provided, typically going live within 5–7 days. Where you have not supplied your own images or logo, we will contact you separately to obtain them; delays in providing these may delay your launch date.

4. Revision period

After launch, you have a revision period during which we make reasonable adjustments to wording, images and layout within the scope of your original questionnaire answers. This is not intended for a full redesign, new pages, or content outside the original brief — changes of that kind are treated as a new small project with its own quote.

5. Ongoing updates (Standard & Pro hosting)

Standard and Pro hosting include minor website updates at no extra charge: text and wording changes, contact details, opening hours, prices, and swapping an existing photo for another you supply, made at a reasonable frequency. This does not cover new photography or image sourcing, new pages or sections, layout or design changes, or very frequent day-to-day editing (for example, multiple changes in a single day) — these are quoted separately as their own fixed-price project.

6. Content you provide

Any text, images, logos or other material you supply to us must be your own, or material you have the right to use. By supplying it, you confirm you have that right and are responsible for its accuracy and legality. We may decline to publish, or may remove, any content that appears unlawful, infringes someone else’s rights, or falls outside the eligibility criteria in Section 2.

7. Payment & billing

The £250 build fee is a one-off payment taken before work begins. Standard and Pro hosting fees are billed monthly in advance via Stripe on a rolling, cancel-anytime basis. We do not store your card details; all payment processing is handled directly by Stripe under their own terms.

8. Changes to hosting fees

We may change our hosting fees for future billing periods, with at least 30 days’ notice by email. If you don’t want to continue at the new price, you can cancel before it takes effect, as set out in Section 9.

9. Cancelling or suspending hosting

You may cancel Standard or Pro hosting at any time. We will give advance notice before your site is taken offline, and hand over your website files so you can move to Self-Hosted or another provider.

We may also cancel or suspend your hosting at any time — for example, for non-payment, misuse, a breach of these terms, or where eligibility no longer applies. Where reasonably possible we’ll give you advance notice first. Either way, we will provide your website files so your site isn’t left stranded, except where cancellation is due to unpaid fees, in which case files are provided once any outstanding balance is settled.

10. Domain names

Where you ask us to register a domain on your behalf, we will confirm availability with you before purchase. Domain registration and renewal costs, where applicable, will be communicated clearly before any charge is made. Where you already own a domain, you remain the registered owner and are responsible for its renewal unless otherwise agreed.

11. Ownership

On the Self-Hosted plan, you receive and own the finished website files outright. On Standard and Pro plans, your website content and branding remain yours; the underlying hosting arrangement remains with us for the duration of your subscription.

12. Liability

We aim to deliver a working, professional website as described in these terms, but we do not guarantee specific business outcomes, search engine rankings, or uninterrupted uptime. We’re not responsible for outages or failures of third-party services we rely on (such as our hosting provider, Stripe, or domain registrars) that are outside our control. Except where liability cannot legally be excluded, our total liability to you in connection with this service is limited to the total fees you’ve paid us in the 12 months before the issue arose.

13. Changes to these terms

We may update these terms from time to time to reflect changes to the service. The current version will always be available on this page.

14. Governing law

These terms are governed by the laws of England and Wales.
