Terms & Conditions

Last updated: July 2026.

1. The service

Support Local Growth designs and builds standard brochure websites for UK small businesses and sole traders. Self-Hosted and Standard carry a flat one-off build fee of £250. Pro waives that build fee entirely under our subsidised scheme, with no minimum term — see Section 5 for how domain and website ownership works on Pro. Standard hosting is £15/month on a rolling, cancel-anytime basis, or you may take the Self-Hosted option and host the finished files yourself at no ongoing cost.

2. Eligibility for Pro’s free website

Pro’s waived build fee is available to businesses that are UK-based, run by the business owner themselves (not a franchise, subsidiary or branch of a larger group), have fewer than 25 employees, and have under £150,000 annual net profit. Full detail is on our Eligibility & FAQ page. Self-Hosted and Standard do not require these criteria and are available to any UK business.

Regardless of plan, we do not build for gambling, adult content, political campaigning, or illegal businesses, and we only build standard informational brochure sites — not ecommerce, booking systems, member logins, or bespoke web applications. We may decline, pause or discontinue an order at any point if a business does not meet the applicable criteria for its plan, including where this only becomes apparent after work has started.

3. Ordering process

You order by completing our build form and choosing a hosting plan. Payment is processed by Stripe. Once payment is confirmed, we build your site to the specifications provided, typically going live within 1–5 days depending on our current workload. Where you have not supplied your own images or logo, we will contact you separately to obtain them; delays in providing these may delay your launch date. For Pro orders, we ask for your full legal name and address as part of registering your domain.

4. Cancelling a website build order (Self-Hosted & Standard)

This section applies to Self-Hosted and Standard, where a £250 build fee is paid upfront. That fee secures your place and work typically begins shortly after payment, so once an order has been placed and paid for, it cannot be cancelled or refunded by you. If you need to change details of your specification after ordering, that’s handled through the revision period after launch, not by cancelling the order.

We may cancel or decline a build order at any point, including after payment, for example where a business falls into one of the excluded categories in Section 2, or for any other reasonable reason. Where we cancel an order, we will refund the £250 build fee in full.

Pro orders work differently, since no build fee is paid upfront — see Section 5.

5. Pro: free website build

Choosing Pro waives the standard £250 website build fee entirely, as part of our subsidised scheme. There is no minimum term and no penalty for cancelling early — you can leave whenever you like. This offer is available to eligible businesses as set out in Section 2.

For the first 12 months of your Pro subscription, your domain name and website remain the property of Support Local Growth — see Sections 12 and 13. If you cancel or stop paying during this period, hosting stops in line with Section 11, and the domain and website remain ours, since ownership was never transferred to you.

Once you’ve been with us for 12 months, both the domain and the website become fully yours. If you choose to move on after that point, we’ll facilitate the transfer as set out in Section 12, and provide your website files.

We may also decline or cancel a Pro order before work begins, for example where a business doesn’t meet the eligibility criteria in Section 2 or for any other reasonable reason; no build fee was paid, so none is refundable.

6. Revision period

After launch, you have a revision period during which we make reasonable adjustments to wording, images and layout within the scope of your original form answers. This is not intended for a full redesign, new pages, or content outside the original brief — changes of that kind are treated as a new small project with its own quote.

7. Ongoing updates (Standard & Pro hosting)

Standard and Pro hosting include minor website updates at no extra charge: text and wording changes, contact details, opening hours, prices, and swapping an existing photo for another you supply, made at a reasonable frequency. This does not cover new photography or image sourcing, new pages or sections, layout or design changes, or very frequent day-to-day editing (for example, multiple changes in a single day) — these are quoted separately as their own fixed-price project. These additional charges are quoted on a case-by-case basis, but we always aim to keep costs as low as possible — helping small businesses grow is the whole point of this scheme.

8. Content you provide

Any text, images, logos or other material you supply to us must be your own, or material you have the right to use. By supplying it, you confirm you have that right and are responsible for its accuracy and legality. We may decline to publish, or may remove, any content that appears unlawful, infringes someone else’s rights, or falls into one of the excluded business categories in Section 2.

9. Payment & billing

Where applicable, the £250 build fee is a one-off payment taken before work begins. Standard and Pro hosting fees are billed monthly in advance via Stripe. We do not store your card details; all payment processing is handled directly by Stripe under their own terms.

10. Changes to hosting fees

Prices you’ve already signed up to don’t change. If we ever adjust our hosting fees, the new pricing applies only to new customers signing up after the change — you’ll continue paying the fee you originally agreed to for as long as you stay with us on that plan.

11. Cancelling or suspending your hosting subscription

You may cancel Standard or Pro hosting at any time, with no penalty. We will give advance notice before your site is taken offline. On Standard and on Pro after your first 12 months, we will hand over your website files so you can move to Self-Hosted or another provider. On Pro within your first 12 months, your domain and website remain ours, as set out in Section 5.

We may also cancel or suspend your hosting at any time — for example, for non-payment, misuse, a breach of these terms, or where eligibility no longer applies. Where reasonably possible we’ll give you advance notice first. Either way, we will provide your website files so your site isn’t left stranded (subject to the Pro ownership timing in Section 5), except where cancellation is due to unpaid fees, in which case files are provided once any outstanding balance is settled.

12. Domain names

Where you ask us to register a domain on your behalf, we will confirm availability with you before purchase, and any registration or renewal costs will be communicated clearly before any charge is made. On Self-Hosted and Standard, we consider that domain to be your property from the point it’s registered.

On Pro, the domain remains our property for the first 12 months of your subscription, alongside the website itself — see Section 5. Once that period is complete, ownership of the domain passes to you automatically, and we will continue to host it for as long as your Pro subscription continues.

On any plan, if you ask us to transfer your domain elsewhere (including a Pro domain once your first 12 months are complete), we will unlock the domain and provide the authorisation (EPP) code needed to complete the transfer within 14 days of you confirming which registrar you’re moving to and requesting the transfer.

Where you already own a domain before joining us, you remain the registered owner throughout on any plan, and are responsible for its renewal unless otherwise agreed.

13. Ownership

On Self-Hosted and Standard, you own your website files and content outright from the point of delivery.

On Pro, your website (and domain, see Section 12) remain the property of Support Local Growth for the first 12 months of your subscription, as set out in Section 5. After that point, ownership passes to you. If you choose to move on at any time after that, we’ll provide your website files and facilitate the domain transfer as described in Section 12.

14. Liability

We aim to deliver a working, professional website as described in these terms, but we do not guarantee specific business outcomes, search engine rankings, or uninterrupted uptime. We’re not responsible for outages or failures of third-party services we rely on (such as our hosting provider, Stripe, or domain registrars) that are outside our control. Except where liability cannot legally be excluded, our total liability to you in connection with this service is limited to the total fees you’ve paid us in the 12 months before the issue arose.

15. Changes to these terms

We may update these terms from time to time to reflect changes to the service. The current version will always be available on this page.

16. Governing law

These terms are governed by the laws of England and Wales.
