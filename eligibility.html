Eligibility & FAQ

Built for genuinely independent UK businesses

This scheme is subsidised, which means it’s targeted. The criteria below exist so the support goes to the local businesses it’s meant for — not larger companies that could pay standard agency rates.

You qualify if you are

  • UK-based

    Your business is registered and operating in the United Kingdom.

  • Independently owned

    Not a franchise, subsidiary, or branch of a larger group.

  • Fewer than 25 employees

    Including yourself, part-time and full-time staff.

  • Under £150,000 annual net profit

    Based on your most recent full financial year.

  • A standard brochure site is right for you

    You need an informational website, not online sales or booking.

We don’t build for

  • Gambling or betting businesses

  • Adult content or services

  • Political campaigning or lobbying organisations

  • Any illegal business or activity

  • Businesses requiring ecommerce, online payments, or a shop

  • Businesses requiring user logins or member accounts

  • Businesses requiring an online booking or reservation system

  • Bespoke web applications or custom-built systems

Frequently asked

Questions we get asked most

What exactly is a “standard brochure website”?
A set of informational pages — typically Home, About, Services, Gallery and Contact — that present your business, what you offer, and how to reach you. It doesn’t include online sales, logins, or booking functionality.
Do you have examples of what my website design will look like?
The clearest example is the site you’re looking at right now. Your own site follows the same structure: a clear top navigation menu, a hero section introducing your business, dedicated sections for what you offer and how to get in touch, a working contact form, and full contact details in the footer. The actual colours, style and layout emphasis are built to your questionnaire answers rather than copied from this design, so your finished site won’t look identical to this one — but it gives you a realistic sense of the structure, professionalism and page count to expect.
How is this actually subsidised — what’s the catch?
There isn’t one. The £250 build fee and hosting prices reflect a standardised process and a fixed scope, rather than bespoke agency pricing. We keep costs down by building to a consistent, well-tested template process rather than starting from scratch on every project, and by restricting the scope to brochure sites.
What if my business is just over the employee or profit threshold?
Get in touch and explain your situation — the thresholds are there to keep the scheme focused on small independents, but we review borderline cases individually.
Do I have to use your hosting?
No. The Self-Hosted tier gives you the finished website files for £250 with no ongoing fee, and you host them wherever you choose. Standard and Pro include hosting, security and support for a monthly fee.
Can I supply my own domain name?
Yes. During the questionnaire you can tell us if you already own a domain, and we’ll connect it. If you don’t have one yet, we’ll help you choose and register one as part of the process.
How long does a revision period last, and what can I change?
The revision period follows launch and covers reasonable adjustments to text, images and layout within the original brief. It’s not intended for a full redesign or new pages outside what was agreed — get in touch if you’re unsure whether a change is in scope.
What counts as a “minor update” under Standard and Pro hosting?

Included, at no extra charge: text and wording changes, contact details, opening hours, prices, and swapping out a photo you’ve already supplied for another one you supply — made at a reasonable frequency. This covers occasional updates, not day-to-day editing: for example, changing your text several times a day isn’t covered under a minor update and would be treated separately.

Not included — quoted separately: new photography or image sourcing, new pages or sections, and layout, structure or design changes. These are treated as small additional projects with their own fixed quote, so you always know the cost before we start.

What happens if I stop paying for hosting?
You’ll get advance notice before anything is taken offline, with the option to move to Self-Hosted and take your files elsewhere.
Is my business definitely eligible if I meet all five criteria?
Meeting the criteria means you’re very likely eligible. We do a final check during the questionnaire stage to confirm before the build fee is taken.
